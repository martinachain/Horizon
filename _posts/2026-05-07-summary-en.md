## [Learning the Integral of a Diffusion Model](https://sander.ai/2026/05/06/flow-maps.html) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Sander Dieleman's blog post introduces a novel perspective on generative modeling by exploring the integral of diffusion models and their connection to flow maps, proposing a method to learn both instantaneous velocity and its integral over finite time intervals simultaneously. This work bridges diffusion models and flow maps, potentially enabling faster inference by compressing iterative sampling into a single forward pass, which could significantly accelerate generative model deployment in applications like image and audio synthesis. The proposed approach combines a consistency-based loss with the standard diffusion loss, allowing the model to learn both the instantaneous velocity and its integral without requiring a pre-trained model for ground truth velocity.

hackernews · benanne · May 6, 18:46 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48040002)

**Background**: Diffusion models generate data by iteratively denoising a random sample through a reverse process, which is computationally expensive. Flow maps, also known as consistency models or shortcut models, aim to learn a direct mapping from noise to data in a single step, greatly speeding up inference. This post explores the theoretical connection between these two approaches by viewing flow maps as the integral of a diffusion model's velocity field.
